\section{Background and Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

Organised efforts to obstruct climate science are extensively documented by investigative platforms: DeSmog~\cite{desmog} profiles individuals and organisations involved in denial and lobbying; LobbyMap~\cite{lobbymap} tracks corporate engagement with climate policy; SourceWatch catalogues front groups and funding relationships. Oreskes and Conway~\cite{oreskes2011merchants} analyse the deliberate strategies used to manufacture doubt about scientific consensus. Despite the rigour of this work, the information remains heterogeneous, siloed, and not interlinked for computational analysis. In the Semantic Web community, CimpleKG~\cite{burel2024cimplekg} and ClimaFactsKG~\cite{burel2025climafactskg} address climate misinformation through structured resources, but are oriented towards claim verification rather than the lobbying actor networks that LACK represents.

Knowledge graphs have been increasingly adopted as infrastructure for investigative journalism. Opdahl et al.~\cite{opdahl2022semantic} survey KG applications across the news production pipeline, identifying actor network analysis as a key but underserved use case; Gallofrè Ocaña and Opdahl~\cite{gallofre2022jkp} find that deeper investigative tasks such as tracing funding relationships remain largely unaddressed. Berven et al.~\cite{berven2020kg} demonstrate the practical viability of KG-based newsroom infrastructure, while Alexander et al.~\cite{alexander2023manifesto} and Motta et al.~\cite{motta2025epistemology} show how formal models can underpin large-scale media analytics. This body of work establishes the value of KGs for journalism, but focuses on news content and event extraction rather than political and corporate actor networks.

The construction of LACK draws on recent advances in LLM-based knowledge graph construction. Trajanoska et al.~\cite{trajanoska2023llmkg} demonstrate that LLMs can drive automated KG construction from unstructured text, outperforming specialised models on entity and relation extraction; Papaluca et al.~\cite{papaluca2024zeroshot} show that LLMs are competitive in zero- and few-shot triplet extraction settings. 

To build the RDF graph with the knowledge extraction output, Façade-X~\cite{asprino2023knowledge} and its SPARQL Anything implementation~\cite{daga2021opinionated} provide a principled method for KG construction from files. 
Ontology design draws on Schema.org~\cite{schemaorg}, PROV-O~\cite{provo}, and DCMI terms~\cite{dcterms} for provenance and metadata, with Wikidata~\cite{wikidata} as the backbone for entity linking. 
Finally, the resource is published following the FAIR principles~\cite{wilkinson2016fair}.

The existing landscape reveals a clear gap: investigative platforms document climate lobbying in depth but resist computational analysis; journalism KGs support news analytics but not political influence networks; and LLM-based KG construction has not been applied to climate lobby networks. 
LACK addresses all three dimensions as a structured, Wikidata-linked knowledge graph of lobbying actors, built via an evaluated extraction pipeline and published as Linked Open Data.
