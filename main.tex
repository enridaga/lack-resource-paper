\begin{abstract}
Organised lobbying against climate science is extensively documented by investigative platforms such as DeSmog and LobbyMap, yet the information they produce remains fragmented, heterogeneous, and difficult to analyse at scale.
We present LACK (Lobbying Against Climate Knowledge), a knowledge graph of approximately 70,000 typed, temporally annotated relations between persons and organisations involved in climate lobbying, constructed from these sources and linked to Wikidata.
LACK is described using a purpose-built OWL ontology, built via an LLM-based relation extraction pipeline, and published as Linked Open Data under CC BY 4.0 following FAIR principles.
We demonstrate its analytical potential through a use case and validate its utility with domain experts from the investigative journalism and climate accountability sector.

\end{abstract}
